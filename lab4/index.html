Banana Bread Recipe

Banana Bread Recipe

1. Gather Ingredients and Supplies

  • 1.1. Measure out:
    • 2 to 3 ripe bananas
    • 1/3 cup melted butter
    • 1 teaspoon baking soda
    • Pinch of salt
    • 3/4 cup sugar (adjust to taste)
    • 1 large egg, beaten
    • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
    • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1.2. Get a loaf pan, a spatula, a fork, and a mixing bowl

2. Preheat the Oven

  • 2.1. Set the oven to 350Â°F to preheat.

3. Prepare the Baking Pan

  • 3.1. Grease a 4x8-inch loaf pan with butter or nonstick spray.

4. Mash the Bananas

  • 4.1. Peel the ripe bananas.
  • 4.2. Place them in a large mixing bowl.
  • 4.3. Mash the bananas using a fork until smooth.

5. Mix Wet Ingredients

  • 5.1. Add the melted butter to the mashed bananas.
  • 5.2. Stir in the sugar, beaten egg, and vanilla extract until combined.

6. Add Dry Ingredients

  • 6.1. Sprinkle the baking soda and a pinch of salt over the wet mixture.
  • 6.2. Stir in the flour gradually until all ingredients are fully combined.

7. Pour the Batter into the Pan

  • 7.1. Pour the banana bread batter into the greased loaf pan.
  • 7.2. Smooth the top of the batter with a spatula if necessary.

8. Bake the Banana Bread

  • 8.1. Place the loaf pan in the preheated oven.
  • 8.2. Bake for 50-60 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.

9. Cool the Bread

  • 9.1. Once the bread is done, remove it from the oven.
  • 9.2. Let the bread cool in the pan for 10 minutes.
  • 9.3. Transfer the bread to a wire rack to cool completely.

10. Serve and Enjoy

  • 10.1. Once the bread has cooled, slice and serve.
  • 10.2. Optionally, spread butter or honey on each slice.
Rock-Paper-Scissors Pseudocode

Pseudocode for Rock-Paper-Scissors Game

  1. Set Up the Game Environment
    1. Display the game title and instructions.
    2. Provide options for the player to choose between "Rock," "Paper," or "Scissors."
  2. Handle Player Input
    1. Wait for the player to select one of the options (Rock, Paper, or Scissors).
    2. Validate the player's input.
  3. Generate the Computer's Choice
    1. Use a random number generator to select Rock, Paper, or Scissors for the computer.
  4. Compare Player and Computer Choices
    1. Determine the outcome based on the following rules:
      • Rock beats Scissors.
      • Scissors beats Paper.
      • Paper beats Rock.
      • Identical choices result in a tie.
  5. Display the Result
    1. Show the player's choice and the computer's choice.
    2. Announce the outcome (Win, Loss, or Tie).
  6. Ask the Player if They Want to Play Again
    1. Provide an option for the player to:
      • Play another round.
      • Quit the game.
  7. Handle End of Game
    1. If the player chooses to quit, display a thank-you message.
